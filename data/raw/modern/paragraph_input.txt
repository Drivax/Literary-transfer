As he was about to leave the room, he remembered that day in April 1995, in a maternity ward in San Diego. The first time he had held his newborn daughter. He was so moved and overwhelmed that he didn’t even know what to do. All he had seen then was a tiny baby with a wrinkled face who, with her eyes closed, was making all sorts of strange faces, waving her little hands about in every direction. At that moment, he had no idea that she would one day occupy such a central place in his life. That this tiny little bundle would become more important to him than the apple of his eye. He had a vague idea that becoming a father would be a radical change in his life, but he had no idea what that meant in terms of love and emotion. He did not yet know that a child could bring him so much joy. Nor that the loss of a child could one day cause him such profound distress. He had no inkling of any of it. Then that tiny, fragile little angel had opened his eyes and looked at him intently, as if to make him understand that he needed him. He had felt overwhelmed, bursting with boundless love. And surely there are no words to describe such happiness.
